The Origins of the "Buzzer Beater"

One of the most exciting moments in basketball is the buzzer-beater, when a player makes a shot just as the game or shot clock expires. While it seems like a common term today, the concept actually came into existence in 1949. The term "buzzer-beater" refers to any basket scored just before the buzzer sounds, often with dramatic effect, and can happen in any of the game four quarters or in overtime. However, did you know the first recorded buzzer-beater in NBA history was by Chris Ford in 1974? Ford, then a player for the Boston Celtics, nailed a game-winning basket right at the buzzer, and it became one of the most memorable plays in NBA lore. This iconic moment set the stage for the countless game-winning shots and clutch moments that fans would celebrate in years to come.
